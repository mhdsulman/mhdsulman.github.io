Time Dependent Quadrapole: The time inroduces rotation.
  • Part I: Rotation of (x,y) followed by rotation of the horizontal velocity (u(x,y),v(x,y)), i.e.
    • Step 1: Rotate (x,y), i.e.   x1 = cos(ω t) * x + sin(ω t) *y,    y1 = -sin(ω t) * x + cos(ω t) * y
    • Step 2: Evaluate the velocity at (x1,y1,z), i.e.&nbps; u1 = u(x1,y1,z)    v1 = v(x1,y1,z)     w = w(x1,y1,z)
    • Step 3: Rotate the resulting horizontal velocity, i.e. u = cos(ω t) * u1 + sin(ω t) *v1,    v = -sin(ω t) *u1 + cos(ω t) *v1.


    • Part II: Rototation of (x,y) without rotating the velocity field (u(x,y),v(x,y)), i.e.
      • Step 1: Rotate (x,y), i.e.   x1 = cos(ω t) * x + sin(ω t) *y,    y1 = -sin(ω t)* x + cos(ω t) y.
      • Step 2: Evaluate the velocity at (x1,y1,z), i.e., u = u(x1,y1,z),    v = v(x1,y1,z),     w = w(x1,y1,z).


    • Part III: Adding rotation to the velocity: xr = cos(ω t) x + sin(ω t) *y,    yr = -sin(ω t) x + cos(ω t) y,   u = u + xr,    v = v + yr.


    • Part IV: Adding rotation to the velocity: xr = ω*(-sin(ω t)*x + cos(ω t)*y),    yr = ω(-cos(ω t)*x - sin(ω t)) y,   u = u + xr,    v = v + yr.
